# Our mission

We exist to bring aerospace stories to Canada and the world — through a Canadian lens, with clear sourcing, and without reducing a booming industry to clickbait.

The universe is expanding. So is humanity's place in it. Rockets are launching from three continents. Canadian tech is showing up on international missions. Startups are announcing things that will reshape how we live in space. But aerospace news is fragmented: buried in press releases, locked behind paywalls, scattered across Reddit and X, or oversimplified for general audiences. **We unify it.** We aggregate, filter, and summarize — and publish the result every morning.

!!! quote "From Here To The Stars"

    We believe aerospace shouldn't feel distant. Whether you're an engineer, a journalist, a student, or just someone who looks up and wonders — you deserve to know what's happening. We publish so you can keep up.

---

## Who this is for

You might find us useful if you are:

- **Aerospace enthusiasts** — space followers who want daily updates beyond Reddit and X
- **Journalists** — reporters looking for a curated jumping-off point on Canada's role in space, NASA missions, or commercial spaceflight
- **Students** — learning about the industry, careers, or engineering
- **Industry professionals** — tracking launches, competitors, and policy
- **Curious Canadians** — people who wonder what Canada's doing in space and why it matters
- **Anyone** who believes space is worth paying attention to

---

## What we do (and don't) do

**We are a news aggregator, not a newsroom.** We don't chase stories, conduct interviews, or break news. What we do — and try to do well — is give you an easy-to-digest snapshot of the aerospace industry through different focus lenses (SpaceX, Rocket Lab, Blue Origin, Canada, NASA, commercial, and more).

The stories we surface come from the aerospace outlets, agencies, and companies that do that reporting. We credit them, link to them, and summarize their work. Then we present it in one place, on a schedule, with a Canadian point of view.
