# How we think about the news

The Canadian Space isn't just a feed. It's a commitment to how we cover aerospace: transparently, fairly, and with respect for your time and intelligence. Here's what we believe — and what we won't do.

---

## AI in the newsroom

We use AI to draft stories. We use AI to fact-check them. We're public about it because we think it's the future of publishing, and hiding it would be dishonest.

AI is fast and tireless. Humans catch nuance, spot bias, and ask "is this right?" Our model works because we do both.

For the full pipeline — every stage from feed to publish — see [The Editorial Pipeline](../how-it-works/pipeline.md). For how we keep quality up, see [Quality Assurance](../how-it-works/quality-assurance.md).

!!! info "Why be transparent?"

    AI-assisted publishing works when readers trust it. Hiding the AI doesn't build trust — transparency does. We tell you when a story was AI-drafted because that's honest, and honest reporting is the only kind worth reading.

---

## What we celebrate (and what we won't)

We celebrate **achievement**. When Rocket Lab launches, when NASA discovers something, when a startup reaches a milestone — we tell you the story. We celebrate Canadian contributions. We celebrate the audacity of trying to live off-Earth.

We **don't hype**. A rocket test isn't "historic" until it actually achieves something. A funding round isn't revolutionary until the company builds. We avoid the adjectives that belong in marketing, not news.

We **don't clickbait**. No misleading headlines. No ALL CAPS. No fake urgency. No speculation dressed up as fact.

We **don't scrape without attribution**. If we're summarizing a story, we link to it. We credit the original source — NASA, SpaceX, Reuters, whoever broke the news.

We **don't cover rumors** unless they come from official channels (like Elon's X announcements, which are de facto official for SpaceX). Reddit speculation about Starship? Not without confirmation.

We **don't do original reporting**. TCS is a news aggregator, not a newsroom. We don't chase leads, run interviews, or break stories — we surface, summarize, and credit the outlets that do.

---

## Corrections policy

We get things wrong sometimes. When we do, we fix it and we tell you:

- **Corrections** are published as updates to the original article, with a timestamp and explanation.
- **Major corrections** (facts that change the meaning of the story) are flagged clearly at the top of the article.
- **Reader-caught errors** — if you catch something, email us with the source. If it's valid, we'll correct and credit you.

Transparency means admitting when you're wrong.

---

## Why this matters

Aerospace is too important to get wrong. Engineers read our site. Journalists cite us. Students learn from our articles. Policymakers might glance at our Canada coverage. That responsibility is real. So we don't take shortcuts. We fact-check. We source. We think about what we publish.

AI makes us fast. Humans make us accurate. That's the TCS difference.
